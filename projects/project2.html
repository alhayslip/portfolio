Final Reflection

All the decisions that I made in the design process were contingent on performing the following data cleaning steps. My design process involved understanding the type of question I wanted to visualize, identifying the segments of data required to capture the data I wanted to see, and deciding on the visualization I wanted to use to showcase the trends in the data effectively. I made decisions such as:

  • I replaced the numerical content in the Month When Complaint Was Received and Month When Complaint Was Closed columns with the name of the month (e.g., 2 was replaced with February).
  • I changed the data type for the Precinct column from object to int64.
  • I dropped null and duplicate records from the original dataframe which contained all of the allegations.
  • I combined the Month When Complaint Was Received and Year When Complaint Was Received columns into one column, Allegation Month and Year.
  • I converted the Allegation Month and Year column into a datetime format using pd.to_datetime().
  • I wrote an np.where statement to define a binary column After Neighborhood Policing, which defined whether the record occurred before or after neighborhood policing was implemented in June 2015.

The most challenging part of this analysis was filtering the original dataframe to ensure that the column for force was created. The intention of doing this was to initiate an np.where statement to create a boolean value based on whether the column “Specific Category of Complaint” contained the phrase “force.” Then, I created a new dataframe titled force, which filtered for all records where the column was set to true. Additionally, I used the isin argument to filter the force dataframe further to segment all records between 2011 and 2019. I chose those years to account for the four years before and after neighborhood policing was implemented.

My conclusion is that a stronger argument can be made that neighborhood policing was not effective in reducing the number of complaints filed. The visualization used in the proposition highlighted that the number of complaints increased after neighborhood policing was implemented. The bar plot for 2015 showed that the number of complaints increased in the months following implementation. While there is evidence that neighborhood policing reduced complaints for excessive force by Black and Hispanic citizens, the data does not indicate a decrease in total complaints overall.

I define ethical analysis and visualization as understanding how to appropriately clean, filter, and visualize data in a way that effectively demonstrates trends and patterns without a hidden agenda. The bounds distinguishing acceptable from misleading design choices are often blurred, but I use the criteria of understanding what type of decision I’m making and its implications to determine ethical versus misleading visualization choices.
