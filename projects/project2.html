Project 2 – Persuasive or Deceptive Visualization?

Alex Hayslip – alhayslip@ucsd.edu

Proposition: Despite the introduction of Neighborhood Policing in 2015, the number of complaints filed did not decrease.

For the Proposition

  • Rationale 1: I created the new binary column "After Neighborhood Policing" to determine if an incident was filed after the program’s implementation in NYC.
  • Rationale 2: I filtered for a dataframe containing data on complaints in 2015 to visualize only that year.
  • Rationale 3: I created a "month_order" list to order the months in ascending order on the x-axis.
  • Rationale 4: I used the hue argument “After Neighborhood Policing” in sns.countplot to separate data before and after the program.
  • Rationale 5: I added plt.legend to distinguish True/False values of “After Neighborhood Policing.”

Against the Proposition

  • Rationale 1: I created a binary column “force” indicating whether a complaint was for excessive force.
  • Rationale 2: I created a new dataframe `force` containing only records where “force” is True.
  • Rationale 3: I filtered for complaints between 2011 and 2019 to compare before (Jan 2011–May 2015) and after (June 2015–Dec 2019) neighborhood policing.
  • Rationale 4: I used the hue argument “After Neighborhood Policing” in sns.countplot to compare differences across ethnic groups.
  • Rationale 5: I used plt.legend to clearly label True/False distinctions for “After Neighborhood Policing.”

Final Reflection

  • My design process began with cleaning and transforming the data to answer specific questions visually.
  • The most challenging step was filtering the data correctly for complaints about excessive force.
  • I was surprised to find that force-related complaints among Black and Hispanic citizens decreased after implementation, though overall complaints didn’t drop.
  • The 2015 barplot was more persuasive, showing an increase in total complaints after implementation.
  • Ethical visualization requires filtering and presenting data truthfully without manipulating reader perception.
